The five categories are not closed clusters separated by sharp boundaries, but analytical levels positioned in a continuum along the actor-focus axis. System-oriented approaches enable thinking about individual, community, and ecosystem actors together, while future-oriented approaches add temporal depth to all four categories.

Four Analytical Axes

57 design approaches were evaluated along four analytical axes. Actor focus was positioned as the primary axis of classification; the other three axes function as sub-variables enabling intra-category positioning.

① Actor Focus (primary)

  • ◉ Individual — physical, cognitive, social
  • ◎ Community — collectives, social networks
  • ◈ Environment — ecosystems, planet
  • ◇ System — socio-technical structures
  • ◆ Future — alternative scenarios

② Intervention Scale

  • Micro — individual product/service
  • Mezzo — community/institution
  • Macro — system/planet

③ Design Mission

  • Enhancing social welfare
  • Ensuring equal participation
  • Creating behavioural change
  • Raising awareness
  • Supporting environmental sustainability
  • Producing systemic transformation

④ Temporal Orientation

  • Short-term
  • Short-to-medium term
  • Medium-term
  • Medium-to-long term
  • Long-term
  • Long-term / uncertain

About This Platform

This open participation platform accompanies the study titled "Thematic Classification of Socially Responsible Design Approaches: A Holistic Framework Proposal."

The study is grounded in Jabareen's (2009) conceptual framework analysis method. The first six phases have been applied; the validation and rethinking phases have been opened to collective participation through this platform.

57 design approaches associated directly or indirectly with socially responsible design were systematically examined along four analytical axes: actor focus, intervention scale, design mission, and temporal orientation.

Five thematic categories emerged: Individual-oriented, Community-oriented, Environment-oriented, System-oriented, and Future-oriented approaches.

Contribution types: New approach suggestion · Category/axis modification · General framework commentary.
